Potato Pumpkin Soup

Description

This creamy potato pumpkin soup is a cozy, autumn-inspired dish that's both nutritious and comforting. It combines the earthiness of potatoes with the mild sweetness of pumpkin, and it’s subtly seasoned with garlic, thyme, and a hint of nutmeg for warmth. Blended until velvety smooth, this soup is perfect for cool weather and can be topped with a swirl of cream or coconut milk for extra richness. Serve with crusty bread for a complete meal that’s sure to become a seasonal favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (fresh or canned)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk (or regular cream)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional garnishes: fresh parsley, pumpkin seeds, swirl of coconut milk or cream

Steps

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the Vegetables: Add the diced potatoes and stir to coat them with the oil and aromatics. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add Pumpkin and Broth: Stir in the pumpkin puree, vegetable broth, dried thyme, and nutmeg. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20–25 minutes, until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, carefully transfer the soup in batches to a blender and blend until creamy. Return the soup to the pot if using a blender.
  5. Add the Cream: Stir in the coconut milk or regular cream and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Let the soup heat through on low for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley, pumpkin seeds, or a swirl of coconut milk for a decorative touch.
  7. Enjoy: Serve hot, alongside crusty bread or a side salad, for a satisfying meal.
